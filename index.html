What is Structural Resonance?

Every structure has natural frequencies and mode shapes. When external forcing (wind, footsteps, vehicles) matches these frequencies, amplitudes grow dramatically.

For a cantilever beam, the first mode natural frequency is:

\[ f_n = \frac{1.875^2}{2\pi L^2} \sqrt{\frac{EI}{\rho A}} \]

Engineering Solutions to Prevent Resonance

Key structural techniques include:

  • Tuned Mass Dampers: secondary mass-spring tuned to cancel primary oscillation (used in skyscrapers and bridges).
  • Structural tuning: modifying stiffness or mass distribution to shift natural frequencies.
  • Material damping: using polymers or hysteretic materials to absorb energy.

Realâ€‘World Applications

  • Seismic isolation in buildings prevents resonance during earthquakes.
